Gyeongbokgung Palace

Background

Built in 1395, Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁), also known as Gyeongbokgung or Gyeongbok Palace, was the main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty (the last dynasty of Korea). It is located in Seoul, South Korea. Gyeongbokgung Palace is where Korea's royalty and government officials resided. Over the years, the palace has been destroyed by the Japanese and other factors, but most of it has now been recovered. In addition to sightseeing, there are many activities visitors can do at Gyeongbokgung Palace.

Gates of the Palace

There are four entrances to the palace. Gwanghwamun Gate (the main and largest gate) is in the south, Yeongchumun Gate is in the west, Sinmumun Gate is in the north, and the entrance of the National Folk Museum of Korea is in the east.

Events

Royal Changing of the Guard

Every hour from 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM, there is a royal changing of the guard ceremony at the main gate, Gwanghwamun. The ceremony is a reenactment of the historical changing of guards of the Joseon dynasty, complete with gurads in uniform, flags, and a gigantic drum. This is a popular and fun event for tourists and locals alike.

*Hanbok

Many visitors like to wear hanbok, traditional Korean clothing, to the palace. There are many hanbok rental shops around the palace.
